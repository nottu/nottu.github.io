Exploiting Cyclic Symmetry in Convolutional Neural Networks

27 Mar 2019

Source

Sander Dielman SEDIELEM@GOOGLE.COM
Jeffrey De Fauw DEFAUW@GOOGLE.COM
Koray Kavukcuoglu KORAYK@GOOGLE.COM


The authors of this paper propose four new neural networks, that can be used together to build CNNs (Convolutional Neural Networks) that are partially or fully rotation equivariant.

Cyclic Symmetry

CNN will often learn multiple copies of the same filter at different orientations. Encoding rotational symmetry into the architecture of the network can reduce the redundancy of learning patterns at different orientations, and can reduce the number of parameters, diminishing the risk of over-fitting.

There are only four possible rotations of the input that would not require interpolation over angles $ k \cdot 90^\text{o}, k \in [0, 1, 2, 3]$. This restricted form of restricted symmetry is what is called cyclic symmetry by the authors of the paper. Applying a horizontal flip, we can get eight possible orientations: dihedral symmetry

Cyclic symmetry can be encoded in CNNs by parameter sharing.
